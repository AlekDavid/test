  THE ANIMATION OF
  SPIDER-MAN: INTO THE SPIDER-VERSE

    “Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse" may at first just seem like a kids’ movie,
    but believe me when I tell you that it forever changed the animation industry.

    This movie is REMARKABLE for its very unique style, as it very specifically searched for the visual approach of a comic-book.
    To obtain this approach, the studio combined both traditional 2D hand-drawn animation techniques, with computer-generated 3D imagery,
     which ultimately led to a never-before-seen visual experience for audiences; and inspired many more artists not just to tag along the new style,
     but also to stretch their minds in the animation realm and create their own.

    The film's success demonstrated that there is a demand for more diverse and innovative animation styles,
    and has paved the way for more experimentation in the industry.
    "Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse" also won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature,
    solidifying its impact on the animation world.


  THE ANIMATION OF
  PUSS IN BOOTS: THE LAST WISH

    From the animation style to its storytelling through color, its fantasy-like style,
    and with its production having started on the same year that “Into The Spider-Verse” was released,
    it’s no great discovery that “Puss in Boots: The Last Wish” was massively influenced by the former.

    Besides its inspiration though, I can confidently say that “Puss In Boots: The Last Wish”
    set the bar so incredibly high up in the entertainment industry.
    The writing and direction of the characters’ journey holds great lessons that despite being in a “kids’ movie”, they easily get into one’s deep
    thoughts. This is achieved not just through great writing, but also through its amazing use of music and atmosphere that put you just right in
    the characters position, thus, making you feel what they feel.
